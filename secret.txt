Independent Study Research Topic and Abstract:

BrainMITMAG: Enhancing Brain Analysis with Multi-Task Instruction Tuning and fMRI-Text Graph Alignment

Juliana Mantebea Danso

Abstract-Using fMRI-derived functional connectivity matrices and their graph representations is a well-established method for brain analysis. However, despite recent advances in multimodal learning, particularly those integrating text, brain analysis tasks still struggle in such settings. This is largely due to the limited availability of textual annotations that contextualize brain activity, as most fMRI datasets primarily consist of time-series data, brain images, and tabular phenotypic information. While these modalities capture brain activity and patient characteristics, they lack rich semantic insights. Integrating context-aware brain activity text, provides rich diagnostic insights and complementary information beyond fMRI and offers a promising addition to multimodal learning for brain analysis. To integrate context-aware text into such multimodal setting, we propose BrainMITMAG, a framework for brain disorder diagnosis comprising two key modules: (1) a Multi-Task Instruction Tuning (MIT) module that generates context-aware brain text by condensing each subject's time-series activations and phenotypic data into structured textual representations, and (2) a Multimodal Alignment of Graph (MAG) module that embeds graph features derived from fMRI data (time-series and image) and textual representations into a shared semantic space using a regularized cosine similarity loss to reduce modality gaps. Extensive experiments on ABIDE, HCP, and ADHD-200 datasets demonstrate that BrainMITMAG outperforms existing methods, achieving improved diagnostic accuracy, lower computational cost, and significant gains in sensitivity, specificity, and ROC-AUC.
